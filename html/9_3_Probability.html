The logic of hypothesis testing: start with a hypothesis that we are not agnostic about. We have a preference. Compute a chance of it being true.

Null Hypothesis: \(H_{0}: \mu = \mu_{0}\) Alternative Hypothesis: \(H_{A}: \mu \neq 0\)

pt command: p-value, from t tables, chance that t is less than 1 standard deviation away from the mean. pt(s,d.f.) multiply pt by two because we’re doing a two-sided test.

pt(-8.12, 14)
## [1] 5.755231e-07
pt(-8.12,14)*2
## [1] 1.151046e-06
