You don’t want to see a pattern in the plot of residualsx fit.

Variation in Y = sum of squares of Y (SSY) = \(\sum_{i-1}^n(Y_{i}-\bar Y )^2\)

** ANOVA identity**

Variation in Y = Variation in \(\hat Y\) + Variation in \(Y-\bar Y\) = \(\sum(Y_{i}-\bar Y)^2\) = \(\sum (\hat Y_{i} - \bar Y)^2 + \sum(Y_{i}- \hat Y_{i})^2\)

\(\sum Y_{i}^2\) = \(\sum \bar Y^2 +\sum(\hat Y_{i}-\bar Y)^2 + \sum(Y_{i}-\hat Y_{i})^2\) -> add the degree of freedom for the mean and the residuals. but this is pretty much never done.

mean squares: Sum of squares divided by the degrees of freedom. mean squares of residual = \(\sigma^2\) Fairer to compare mean squares than sum of squares

** F Value** signal to noise ratio. Expect it to be equal to 1 if it’s all noise. HIgher F value suggests a stronger, more clear signal.
